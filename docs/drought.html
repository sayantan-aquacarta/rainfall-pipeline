Drought Monitor — SPI & LPA

The Standardized Precipitation Index (SPI) measures rainfall deficit or surplus relative to historical variability — it is the internationally recommended drought index (WMO, 2012). Combined with IMD's Long Period Average (LPA) baselines, this module will provide the scientific drought intelligence needed for precision agri-advisory, water resource planning, and climate risk assessment.

W0 Abnormally Wet (+0.5 to +0.8)
W1 Moderately Wet (+0.8 to +1.3)
W2 Severely Wet (> +1.3)

SPI is calculated over rolling windows of 1, 3, 6, and 12 months. SPI-1 detects short-term agricultural drought; SPI-12 captures long-term hydrological drought affecting groundwater and reservoirs.
